# Bradford County United Way 2026 Grant Application
**Urban Ark Conservation Inc.**

---

## Dear Bradford County United Way Board and Community Partners,

**From the bottom of our hearts, thank you.** Your $7,000 investment in Urban Ark Conservation in 2025 didn't just fund infrastructure—it planted seeds of community that blossomed beyond our wildest expectations. As first-time recipients, you took a chance on our vision, and we're proud to report that we stretched every dollar into maximum community benefit.

### **2025 RESULTS: We Delivered More Than Promised**

When we received your generous $7,000 grant, we committed to building aquaponics greenhouse infrastructure for education and community training. We delivered on that promise—and so much more:

**Infrastructure Completed:**
- Greenhouse frames installed with complete site preparation
- Foundation laid for our aquaponics education facility  
- Farm store fully completed with insulation, electrical, and flooring—now operational and serving local customers

**Community Impact Achieved:**
In October 2025, we launched our first-ever **"Casks and Cauldrons"** community celebration. This FREE fall equinox event welcomed all Bradford County residents for an evening of farm-to-fork dining prepared by our chef using produce grown right here on Viall Hill Road. With food trucks, local cider and winery vendors, live music, and community firepits under the stars, approximately **100 neighbors joined us** for what many called "exactly what Bradford County needed."

The photos and stories shared on social media told the real story: families laughing around firepits, kids meeting our heritage Arapawa goats, veterans finding peace in the farm setting, and neighbors connecting across generations. We didn't just host an event—we established Urban Ark Conservation as a true community gathering place for rural Bradford County.

### **2026 VISION: Growing Community Through Solstice Traditions**

Building on 2025's success, we're requesting **$15,000-$20,000** to expand our impact through a comprehensive **Solstice Community Events** initiative that connects Bradford County residents to the natural rhythms of agricultural life while addressing core United Way focus areas.

**THE SOLSTICE CALENDAR:**

**Fall Equinox - "Casks and Cauldrons" 2026**
Our signature harvest celebration returns with expanded capacity for 200+ attendees. This annual tradition celebrates the harvest season with locally-sourced food, Bradford County beverages, live entertainment, and the community firepits that made 2025 so special. It's become our "mark your calendar" moment that brings neighbors together before winter.

**Spring Equinox - NEW Family Celebration**  
We're launching a complementary spring awakening event focused on families and children. Picture baby goat kids born in February and March greeting excited children, hands-on seed starting workshops, community garden plot signups, and the joy of kicking off the growing season together. This daytime celebration will serve as Bradford County's welcome-back-to-spring tradition.

### **FUNDING REQUEST BREAKDOWN**

**1. Complete Aquaponics Greenhouse - $7,000**
Finish what your 2025 investment started: tanks, grow beds, plumbing systems, and initial fish stock. This completes our educational facility for year-round programming and school visits.

**2. Solstice Community Events - $5,000**  
Event supplies, entertainment, promotion, and logistics support for both fall and spring celebrations. This investment creates lasting traditions for Bradford County.

**3. Veteran Agricultural Therapy Program - $4,000**
Formalize therapeutic programming for veterans and first responders, partnering with VA services and veteran organizations. Farm work and animal interaction provide grounding therapy for PTSD, anxiety, and depression.

**4. Youth Education Initiative - $4,000**
School field trip programming, 4-H partnership development, and STEM education integration using our EDEN farm management AI system.

### **United Way Focus Areas Addressed**

**HEALTH:** Our veteran and first responder therapy programming addresses mental health through purposeful farm work and animal interaction in a peaceful rural setting. The therapeutic value of working with heritage livestock and growing food provides natural healing for combat trauma and occupational stress.

**YOUTH OPPORTUNITIES:** Both solstice events center families and children, while our educational programming brings STEM learning to life through aquaponics and farm technology. Kids see career possibilities in sustainable agriculture and conservation.

**FINANCIAL SECURITY:** Our farm store keeps food dollars circulating locally while providing training in sustainable growing techniques. Community members gain practical skills in food production, and local food access improves family food security.

**COMMUNITY RESILIENCE:** Heritage breed conservation (we maintain 15 Arapawa goats—part of only 295 in the entire United States) preserves agricultural genetics for future generations. Our solstice events build social connections that strengthen community bonds, while local food production enhances regional resilience.

### **Sustainability and Stewardship**

We're building toward long-term sustainability through diverse revenue streams: farm store sales, modest event vendor fees, and voluntary donations from community members who value what we're creating. Your grant funding accelerates community benefit while we grow toward self-sustaining operations.

As proven stewards of community investment, we transformed your $7,000 into infrastructure, a major community event, and an operational retail space that serves neighbors year-round. With expanded funding, we'll create lasting traditions that bring Bradford County together across seasons and generations.
