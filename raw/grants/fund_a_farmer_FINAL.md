### ANIMAL WELFARE IMPACT

**Current Challenges:**
- Manual feeding is time-intensive and inconsistent
- Feed storage exposed to weather and pests
- Limited shelter options during pasture rotation
- Barn doors damaged, creating drafts and security issues
- Water systems require constant manual monitoring

**How This Project Improves Animal Welfare:**

1. **Consistent Nutrition:** Automated grain delivery ensures animals receive proper feed amounts on schedule, even when farm operators are unavailable.

2. **Feed Quality:** Enclosed bulk bin protects grain from moisture, mold, and rodents - critical for animal health.

3. **AI Monitoring:** Our EDEN (Ecological Data & Environmental Network) system will monitor feed levels, track consumption patterns, and alert to anomalies that may indicate health issues.

4. **Weather Protection:** Additional run-in shelters allow animals to escape extreme weather during rotational grazing.

5. **Predator Protection:** Repaired barn doors and improved lighting protect animals overnight, especially critical for pregnant does and newborn kids.

6. **Water Security:** Reliable water access prevents dehydration stress, particularly important in winter months.

**Innovation:** This project demonstrates how small farms can leverage technology to improve animal welfare. EDEN's monitoring creates a model replicable by other small-scale conservation farms.
