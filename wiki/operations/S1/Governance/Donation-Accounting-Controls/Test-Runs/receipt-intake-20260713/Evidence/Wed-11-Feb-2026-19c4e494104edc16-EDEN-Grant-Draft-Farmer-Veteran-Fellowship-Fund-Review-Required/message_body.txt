Dear Review Committee,

On behalf of Urban Ark Conservation, I respectfully submit this application for the Farmer Veteran Fellowship Fund seeking $5,000 to expand our heritage breed conservation operations while developing agricultural therapy programming for veterans and first responders in rural Bradford County, Pennsylvania.

As a retired First Sergeant with over two decades of military service, I founded Urban Ark Conservation in 2024 to address two critical needs: the preservation of endangered livestock genetics and the provision of meaningful agricultural therapy opportunities for those who have served. Our organization maintains critical breeding populations of Arapawa goats (representing approximately 4% of the US population), Highland cattle, Gloucestershire Old Spots pigs, and heritage poultry breeds.

Bradford County hosts 4,200 veterans (70 per 1,000 residents—significantly above national averages) in a predominantly rural area (85% rural) where agricultural traditions run deep with 1,400 active farms. Yet resources for veteran mental health and agricultural conservation remain limited. This fellowship will enable us to bridge that gap by enhancing our infrastructure to safely accommodate therapeutic programming while maintaining our conservation mission.

The requested funds will support critical fencing improvements, predator protection systems, and feed storage infrastructure that will allow us to launch our pilot agricultural therapy cohort in summer 2026 while ensuring the welfare of our rare breed populations.
