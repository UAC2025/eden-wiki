# Summary: Arapawa Goat Breed Standard

**Conservation Status:** Critical (The Livestock Conservancy)
(source: raw/animals/2026-06-07-arapawa-goat-breed-standard.md)

This document provides a comprehensive profile of the Arapawa goat, a critically endangered heritage breed originating from Arapawa Island in New Zealand.

## History and Origin
The Arapawa goat is descended from domestic goats brought to New Zealand by European explorers as early as the 1600s, with historical links to Captain James Cook's 1777 expeditions. While once thought to be descended from "Old English" goats, DNA evidence reveals a unique identity and distant relation to South African breeds (source: raw/animals/2026-06-07-arapawa-goat-breed-standard.md). 

In the 1970s, the New Zealand Forest Service attempted to remove the goats to protect native forests. Betty and Walt Rowe, along with volunteers, established a conservation program in 1987 with 40 goats returned to domestication to preserve the breed. As of 2011, the global population was extremely small, with roughly 150 to 200 in the United States (source: raw/animals/2026-06-07-arapawa-goat-breed-standard.md).

## Characteristics
- **Size and Weight:** Medium-sized; Does weigh 60–80 lbs, Bucks up to 125 lbs.
- **Appearance:** Long hair (mostly black, brown, and white), often with facial badger stripes. Typically horned.
- **Reproduction:** Twins are common with little birthing difficulty; excellent mothering skills.
- **Temperament:** Alert, docile, and non-aggressive. Good for families and children, but are known as escape artists requiring secure fencing. 
- **Utility:** Primarily meat, some dairy, and excellent at brush clearing. Hardy and adaptable, though they require shelter from very wet weather.
(source: raw/animals/2026-06-07-arapawa-goat-breed-standard.md)

## Genetics
A 2007 DNA analysis conducted by The Livestock Conservancy, the University of Cordoba, and breeders confirmed that Arapawa goats are clearly distinct from other breeds, making them a unique genetic resource (source: raw/animals/2026-06-07-arapawa-goat-breed-standard.md).
